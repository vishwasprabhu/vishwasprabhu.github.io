SRM checks are typically run at α = 0.001 because they're run repeatedly during an experiment and false alarms are costly.

What is SRM? Sample Ratio Mismatch is when the observed split between variants differs from what was specified (e.g. you configured 50/50 but see 51.2/48.8 with millions of users). It usually signals a bug — bot filtering, redirect issues, sticky bucketing failures, or logging gaps — and invalidates the experiment results.

Method: Pearson chi-squared goodness-of-fit test. Test statistic χ² = Σ (Oᵢ − Eᵢ)² / Eᵢ, with k − 1 degrees of freedom for k variants.

What to do on SRM: Don't ship the experiment. Investigate logging, assignment, and filtering pipelines before trusting the metrics.
